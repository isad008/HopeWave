Clean Water for Rural Kenya Villages

Solar-powered boreholes are being drilled across 12 remote villages in Turkana County, Kenya — the most water-stressed region in sub-Saharan Africa. Once complete, 30,000 people will have access to safe, clean water year-round, eliminating the 6-hour daily walk that currently prevents thousands of children — mostly girls — from attending school.

"Before the borehole, my daughter Fatuma walked six hours every day for water. Now she goes to school every morning. I never thought I would see this day."

— Halima A., Villager, Turkana County, Kenya

Project Impact

30,000 people gain access to safe water within 90 days of completion
94% reduction in waterborne illness in pilot villages
Girls' school attendance up 68% in communities with completed boreholes
Community-trained pump operators ensure long-term maintenance and ownership
Solar panels power pumps indefinitely — zero fuel costs for communities
