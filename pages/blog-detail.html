“We Haven’t Walked 6 Hours for Water in 3 Months”

Home/Blog
Water & Sanitation

Published 15 June 2026 · By Amara Okonkwo, Programs Director · 7-minute read

Fatuma was 11 years old when she stopped going to school. Not because she wanted to. Not because her parents didn’t care. But because someone had to collect the water.

In Namoruputh village, Turkana County, Kenya, the nearest safe water source was a 6-hour round trip on foot. For most families in the village, that task fell to girls and women. Fatuma was one of them — and so were 340 other school-age girls across the 12 villages where HopeWave’s water project is now complete.

“Before the borehole, my daughter walked every morning before sunrise and came back exhausted by noon. Now she leaves for school at 7am and comes home with homework. I never thought I would see this day.”

— Halima A., Fatuma’s mother, Namoruputh Village, June 2026

What Your Donations Built

With your support, our field team — working alongside local engineers and community leaders — drilled and completed 12 solar-powered boreholes across Turkana County. Each borehole is maintained by a community-trained pump operator who earns a small wage from a village fund. The system is designed to be self-sustaining indefinitely.

30,000 people now have year-round access to safe drinking water
94% reduction in waterborne illness reported in pilot villages within 8 weeks
Girls’ school attendance up 68% in villages with completed boreholes
12 community pump operators trained and employed

Fatuma’s Update

We met Fatuma again in June 2026, three months after the borehole in her village was completed. She’s now in Year 7. Her favourite subject is mathematics. She wants to be a civil engineer. “I want to build things that help people,” she told us. “Like someone built our water pump.”

That someone was you.
